## German-Specific Context

### How the Bundestag processes letters

- MdBs receive **hundreds of letters and emails per week** during session weeks
- **Handwritten letters are categorically treated differently** — they reach the MdB personally; email often goes to staff
- Letters from **citizens in the MdB's own Wahlkreis** are prioritized — the MdB is accountable to these constituents at election time
- During **Sitzungswochen** (session weeks), response time is slow — timing to Nicht-Sitzungswochen is better for substantive engagement
- The **Wahlkreisbüro** (local office) is often more accessible than the Berlin office for handwritten letters
- Email addresses follow the pattern: `vorname.nachname@bundestag.de`

### German vs. American advocacy letter style

| German | American |
|--------|----------|
| Sachlich (matter-of-fact) | Emotional storytelling |
| Structured argument | Narrative arc |
| Sie-Form formal distance | First-name familiarity common |
| Facts + personal impact | Personal story + call to action |
| Kompromissbereitschaft | Binary win/lose framing |
| No party attacks | Partisan framing common |

**Key insight:** German political culture rewards *informed engagement*, not passion. A letter that shows the writer understands the complexity of the issue is taken more seriously than one that only expresses frustration.

## Universal Persuasion Principles (Research-Backed)

### 1. Personal story > statistics
"Officials are more often swayed by personal stories than by impersonal statistics." (Community Tool Box)
- Lead with: how this affects *you*, in *your street*, in *your life*
- Statistics can support, but never lead

### 2. One topic, one ask
- Letters with multiple issues get filed as "complex" and deprioritized
- The concrete ask must be **actionable**: not "do something" but "vote for X", "raise this in committee", "meet with us"

### 3. Acknowledge complexity
- Showing you understand why the issue is difficult signals seriousness
- Writers who acknowledge counterarguments are treated as credible partners

### 4. Position the politician as someone who CAN help
- Not: "Sie haben versagt" (creates defensiveness)
- Yes: "Sie haben als Mitglied des Ausschusses für X die Möglichkeit, hier etwas zu bewirken"

### 5. Start with common ground
"Start with something positive — a small compliment or something you have in common increases their willingness to read" (briefe-an-die-politik.org)
- Reference a recent statement or vote you agree with, if possible
- Establishes dialogue frame rather than complaint frame

### 6. Make it local
- "In meiner Straße in [Wahlkreis]..." beats any national statistic
- Local specificity proves you're a real constituent, not a form letter

### 7. Offer to continue the conversation
- "Ich stehe Ihnen gerne für ein Gespräch zur Verfügung"
- Turns the letter into a dialogue offer — more memorable than a one-way message

### 8. Timing windows (for v2 — can't control from app currently)
Most impactful moments to send:
- **Before a vote** on related legislation
- **During budget process** (Haushaltsberatungen, usually autumn)
- **Before elections** (MdBs most responsive to constituents)
- **After a related scandal or news event** (issue is "live")

## Party-Aware Argumentation Guide

Use the politician's party to choose which aspects of an issue to emphasize. The goal is not to adopt the party's position — it's to make the issue *anschlussfähig* (connectable) to their value system.

### SPD
- Frame around: soziale Gerechtigkeit, Arbeitnehmerrechte, Gleichheit, Daseinsvorsorge
- Language: "Gute Arbeit", "sozialer Zusammenhalt", "faire Chancen"
- Avoid: market-efficiency framing

### Grüne
- Frame around: Nachhaltigkeit, Klimaschutz, Generationengerechtigkeit, Lebensqualität
- Language: "Zukunftsfähig", "ökologisch verantwortlich", "lebenswert"
- Referencing the party's own programs works well

### CDU/CSU
- Frame around: Wirtschaftlichkeit, Sicherheit, Ordnung, Eigenverantwortung, Standortvorteil, Bewahrung des Bewährten
- Language: "Verlässlichkeit", "wirtschaftliche Vernunft", "Standortsicherung"
- Avoid: collectivist or redistributive framing

### FDP
- Frame around: individuelle Freiheit, Bürokratieabbau, Innovation, Eigenverantwortung, Effizienz
- Language: "Entbürokratisierung", "Eigeninitiative", "schlanker Staat"
- Avoid: state-intervention framing

### AfD
- Frame: sachlich, demokratisch, lokal, konkret
- Focus on: day-to-day lived experience, local infrastructure, concrete problems
- Avoid: ideological language either direction
- Do NOT dignify extremist positions — stay on the concrete issue only

### BSW
- General sachliche Argumentation, focus on concrete issue
- Economic and social policy framing resonates

### Linke
- Frame around: soziale Ungleichheit, öffentliche Infrastruktur, Umverteilung
- Language: "öffentliches Gut", "gemeinwohlorientiert", "für alle zugänglich"

## Structure of a High-Impact German Political Letter

```
[Datum: TT.MM.JJJJ]

[Absender: Name, Straße, PLZ Ort]
[Empfänger: Titel Vorname Nachname MdB, Ggf. Ausschuss/Funktion, Platz der Republik 1, 11011 Berlin]

[Betreff: optional but helpful — 1 short sentence naming the issue]

Sehr geehrte/r [Titel] [Nachname],

Absatz 1 — Einleitung und persönlicher Bezug (3-4 Sätze)
- Wer Sie sind (kurz: Bürger aus Ihrem Wahlkreis)
- Warum Sie schreiben (das konkrete Anliegen, lokal verankert)
- Optional: Etwas Positives/Gemeinsames voranstellen

Absatz 2 — Das Problem mit Einordnung (4-5 Sätze)
- Das Kernproblem, sachlich beschrieben
- Persönliche/lokale Auswirkungen (nicht abstrakt)
- Komplexität anerkennen (zeigt Ernsthaftigkeit)
- Ggf. Fakten aus seriösen Quellen (1-2, nicht mehr)

Absatz 3 — Der Appell und die Vision (3-4 Sätze)
- Was konkret Sie erwarten (1 Bitte, handlungsorientiert)
- Positive Vision: was wäre der gewünschte Zustand?
- Dialogangebot und Bitte um Antwort

Mit freundlichen Grüßen,
[Name]
```

**Format rules:**
- Max 1 Seite DIN A4 / 200-280 Wörter
- Eigene Worte — keine Floskeln, keine Zitate
- Korrekte Anrede und Rechtschreibung
- Komplette Kontaktdaten des Absenders

## For v2: Tone Slider Implementation

The tone spectrum from "freundlich" to "nachdrücklich" maps to:

| Level | Label | Prompt modifier |
|-------|-------|-----------------|
| 1 | Freundlich-sachlich | "Ich bitte Sie, dieses Thema in Ihre Überlegungen einzubeziehen." |
| 2 | Bestimmt (default) | "Ich erwarte, dass Sie sich für X einsetzen." |
| 3 | Nachdrücklich | "Es ist dringend notwendig, dass Sie als mein Abgeordneter hier klar Stellung beziehen und handeln." |

Data to fetch from Abgeordnetenwatch API after politician selection:
- `committee_memberships[]` — which Ausschüsse they sit on (highly relevant for framing)
- `votes_and_statements` — recent votes on related issues
- `profile_url` — link to full profile
- `candidacies[].electoral_district` — confirm Wahlkreis match

Bundestag.de additional sources:
- `bundestag.de/abgeordnete/{id}` — biography, recent speeches, Abstimmungsverhalten
- Reden im Plenum on related topics

1. After `findPoliticiansByPlz()` resolves the Wahlkreis and selects politician
