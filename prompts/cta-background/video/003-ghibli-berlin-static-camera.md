## Prompt

```
A single, perfectly still wide shot of a solarpunk Berlin neighborhood in soft
afternoon light. The camera does not move at all — this is a painted scene that
breathes.

COMPOSITION:
Wide 16:9 landscape format. We are looking at a quiet Berlin street scene from
a slightly elevated perspective — as if sitting on a second-floor balcony. In
the far background, soft and slightly hazy, the silhouette of the Fernsehturm
rises above the rooftops, catching gentle golden light. The Brandenburger Tor
may be barely suggested even further back, almost dissolving into warm mist.
The middle ground shows the tops of classic Berlin Altbau buildings — but
reimagined: rooftops covered in wildflowers, moss, and small solar panels.
Vines climb the facades. A small wind turbine turns almost imperceptibly on
one roof. The foreground has the canopy of a large linden tree (Berlins
signature tree) with detailed leaves, and below it a glimpse of a cobblestone
street with a red German Briefkasten (mailbox) near the tree trunk.

WHAT MOVES (all very slow, ambient, and subtle):
- A few leaves detach from the linden tree and drift slowly downward,
  tumbling gently in the air
- Two or three birds (swallows or sparrows) glide lazily across the distant
  sky between the buildings, appearing and disappearing naturally
- In the far background, one or two tiny silhouettes of people walk slowly
  along the street — barely noticeable, like figures in a Hiroshige woodblock
  print. One might be on a bicycle
- The wildflowers on the rooftops sway almost imperceptibly, as if touched
  by the faintest breeze
- Soft light shifts very gradually, as if a thin cloud is slowly passing —
  the shadows breathe slightly
- A thin wisp of steam or smoke rises gently from a chimney or café below
- The small wind turbine on the rooftop rotates extremely slowly
- Nothing else moves. The buildings, the tree trunk, the mailbox, the
  cobblestones — all perfectly still

WHAT DOES NOT MOVE:
- The camera. Completely locked off. No pan, no zoom, no drift
- The buildings, streets, and major structures
- The Fernsehturm in the background — it is a still, soft landmark

STYLE & AESTHETIC:
Japanese anime aesthetic, specifically inspired by Studio Ghibli background art
(think: Kazuo Oga's painted backgrounds for My Neighbor Totoro and Kiki's
Delivery Service). Hand-painted feeling with visible brushwork and soft texture.
Watercolor washes for the sky and distant buildings. More detailed gouache-like
rendering in the mid-ground. The look is warm, organic, and crafted — clearly
illustrated, never photorealistic. European architecture drawn through a
Japanese animation sensibility — the way Ghibli depicts European cities in
Kiki's Delivery Service or Howl's Moving Castle, but grounded in real Berlin
Altbau proportions and details.

COLOR PALETTE:
- Sky: soft warm gradient from pale gold near the horizon to a gentle
  blue-green higher up, like late afternoon in early summer
- Buildings: warm sandstone tones, muted terracotta, cream-colored facades
  with the patina of age
- Vegetation: dominant — rich but soft forest greens (#2D6A4F range),
  sage greens, olive tones. The green should feel lush but not neon
- Accents: the red mailbox (#C1121F, muted), a few warm red and orange
  wildflowers on the rooftops, a distant warm window glow
- Light: golden hour warmth without being orange — more like honey-tinted
  daylight
- Overall tone: muted, warm, slightly desaturated — like a memory of the
  most beautiful day in Berlin
- EDGES: The entire frame has soft, feathered edges that gradually dissolve
  into warm cream / off-white (#FAF8F5). No hard borders anywhere. The
  image gently vignettes into nothingness at all four sides, especially
  left, right, and bottom — so it melts seamlessly into a cream-colored
  website background

MOOD & FEELING:
Quiet optimism. The feeling of sitting still and watching a city that works —
where nature and people coexist gently. Not utopian in a sci-fi way, but in
a "what if we just took better care?" way. It should make you take a slow
breath and feel that things could be okay. The warmth of Ghibli's humanism
applied to Berlin.

TECHNICAL:
- Seamless loop, 15-20 seconds
- 16:9 landscape format, high resolution (minimum 1920x1080)
- No sound
- No text, no UI elements, no watermarks
- All ambient movements must loop seamlessly — birds re-enter from where
  they exited, leaves continue to fall, figures continue to walk
- The light shift should be subtle enough to loop without a visible reset
```
