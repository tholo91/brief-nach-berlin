## Prompt

```
SCENE — SINGLE STATIC WIDE SHOT, STUDIO GHIBLI STYLE

A wide 16:9 painted landscape of an idealized, green Berlin — unmistakably
the city, but reimagined as a lush, car-free solarpunk utopia. The camera
is completely fixed. Nothing about the framing changes. This is a living
painting.

COMPOSITION (foreground to background):

FOREGROUND (bottom third):
- The crown of a large linden tree (Berlin's signature tree) fills the
  bottom-left corner, its leaves rendered in Ghibli's signature detailed
  botanical style — multiple shades of green with visible individual leaves
- A quiet cobblestone street stretches across the lower portion, with
  wildflowers growing between the stones
- A classic red German Briefkasten (mailbox) stands near the tree — small,
  iconic, still
- A wooden bench with nobody sitting on it, maybe a forgotten book on it
- Warm afternoon light casts long, soft shadows across the cobblestones

MIDDLE GROUND (center):
- Classic Berlin Altbau buildings line the street — cream and sandstone
  facades, tall windows, ornate but weathered balconies
- Every rooftop is alive: wildflower meadows, small vegetable gardens,
  solar panels, a rooftop café with string lights (unlit — it's daytime)
- Vines and ivy climb several facades in that beautiful unruly Berlin way
- A small canal or section of the Spree cuts through, with clear green
  water reflecting the buildings
- No cars anywhere. Bicycle racks with colorful bikes. A few potted plants
  on the sidewalk outside what looks like a Späti

BACKGROUND (top third):
- The glass dome (Kuppel) of the Reichstag/Bundestag building is clearly
  visible, catching soft light — it's the unmistakable Berlin landmark,
  rendered in Ghibli's soft watercolor distance style, slightly hazy
- The Fernsehturm (TV Tower) rises behind it, further away, even softer
- Two or three elegant modern wind turbines stand among the rooftops in the
  mid-distance — they don't exist in real Berlin, and that's the point.
  This is Berlin as it could be
- The sky is a Ghibli masterpiece: soft gradient from warm golden-peach
  near the horizon to a gentle cerulean blue above, with a few enormous,
  slow, beautifully painted cumulus clouds

WHAT MOVES (all animations are extremely slow and subtle):

1. THE LINDEN TREE: Individual leaves shift and flutter very gently, as if
   touched by the softest breeze. Two or three leaves slowly detach and
   drift down across the frame over the full 15-20 second loop. Their paths
   are long, lazy arcs — not falling, floating.

2. A SINGLE BICYCLE: One person on a bicycle crosses the street from right
   to left, very far in the middle ground, moving slowly and peacefully.
   They enter from the right edge and exit the left edge over the full
   duration of the loop. Small, not a focal point — you notice them after
   a few seconds.

3. THE WIND TURBINES: The blades rotate extremely slowly — barely
   perceptible. One full rotation takes the entire loop duration.

4. A FLOCK OF BIRDS: A small group of 5-7 birds (starlings or swallows)
   moves together across the distant sky, high up near the clouds. They
   drift from one side to the other in a natural, slightly undulating
   formation. They should be small — just dark specks against the sky,
   the way real distant birds look.

5. THE WATER: The canal/Spree surface has the subtlest shimmer — tiny
   shifts in the reflections, barely animated. Like looking at real water
   from a distance.

6. THE CLOUDS: One or two of the large clouds drift almost imperceptibly.
   Over the full loop, they move maybe 2-3% of the frame width — so slow
   you wouldn't notice unless you watched for 30+ seconds.

7. A FEW PEOPLE (2-3 maximum): Tiny figures in the mid-distance. One
   person walks very slowly along the canal. Another stands on a balcony
   and shifts weight slightly. They are not the focus — they are ambient
   life, like figures in a Ghibli wide shot. No facial detail, just
   silhouettes with a hint of clothing color.

WHAT DOES NOT MOVE:
- The camera — completely locked, no drift, no shake, no zoom
- All buildings, the street, the mailbox, the bench, the bicycle racks
- The Reichstag dome, the Fernsehturm
- The tree trunk and major branches (only leaves move)

SEAMLESS LOOP REQUIREMENTS (critical):
- First frame and last frame must be pixel-identical for static elements
- All moving elements must complete full cycles: the bicycle exits left
  and re-enters right at the exact same position and speed, the birds
  complete their crossing and re-enter, falling leaves are replaced by
  new ones detaching at the same positions
- Cloud positions at frame 1 = cloud positions at final frame
- Wind turbine blade angle at frame 1 = blade angle at final frame
  (complete full rotations only)
- The light does NOT shift — constant soft afternoon golden hour
  throughout, to avoid any visible loop point
- Water shimmer pattern repeats seamlessly

STYLE & RENDERING:
- Studio Ghibli background art aesthetic — specifically the style of
  background artist Kazuo Oga (My Neighbor Totoro, Princess Mononoke,
  Kiki's Delivery Service)
- Hand-painted appearance: visible brushstrokes, watercolor washes for
  sky and distant elements, thicker gouache-like rendering for mid-ground
  buildings
- Rich, layered greens — this is the dominant color. Forest green, sage,
  olive, emerald, moss. Every surface that can be green, is green
- Warm but not oversaturated. The palette feels like a slightly faded
  beautiful postcard
- Atmospheric perspective: background elements are softer, hazier, cooler
  in tone. Foreground is sharper and warmer
- The overall image should feel like a frame from a Ghibli film you
  haven't seen yet — warm, humanistic, detailed, alive

COLOR PALETTE:
- Greens (dominant): forest green (#2D6A4F), sage, olive, emerald, moss —
  layered and varied, never flat
- Sky: warm peach-gold at horizon fading to soft cerulean
- Buildings: cream, warm sandstone, muted terracotta, aged white
- Accents: the red mailbox (muted #C1121F), a few warm-toned flowers,
  one yellow bicycle in a rack
- Light: golden afternoon, warm but gentle — honey, not orange
- Shadows: soft blue-green, never black

EDGES & DIMMING (for use as website background):
- The entire image has soft, feathered edges that dissolve into warm
  cream (#FAF8F5) — especially strong on the left, right, and bottom
  edges. The top can be slightly less feathered
- The overall brightness is slightly reduced (roughly 70-80% opacity
  equivalent) — dim enough that white or light-colored text and UI
  elements placed over it remain fully legible
- Think of it as looking at this beautiful scene through a soft cream-
  tinted veil — all the detail is there, but it doesn't fight for
  attention with foreground content
- No hard borders or frame edges anywhere

MOOD:
This is Berlin on its best possible day in a world where things worked
out. Green, quiet, human-scale, hopeful. Not futuristic — just cared for.
The kind of city you'd want to write a letter to protect.

TECHNICAL SPECS:
- Seamless loop, 15-20 seconds
- 16:9 landscape, minimum 1920x1080, ideally 4K
- No sound
- No text, no UI elements, no watermarks
- Gentle film grain overlay (optional) for that hand-drawn warmth
- Export as MP4 (H.264) with high quality for web embedding
```
